Getting Started

What is this Online Vehicle-Pass Registration System?
This is an online platform for all students, faculty, and higher education institution staff to register their motor vehicles for campus access and acquire a vehicle pass sticker.
Who needs to register their vehicle?
All Administrators, faculty, staff, and students who wish to bring their vehicles inside the University campus must register.
How do I create an account?
Simply click on the "Create Free Account" button on the homepage. You will be guided through a simple registration process where you'll need to provide your corporate email and follow the on-screen instructions.
What are the requirements for a vehicle pass application
For a vehicle pass, you must fill out a form with your personal and vehicle information and upload digital copies or clear photos of the following documents: First Part – Personal Information:
  • Full name, address, contact number, and email
  • For Employees: indicate position and office/college
  • For Students: indicate college program, year level, and contact details of father, mother, and guardian
Second Part – Vehicle Information:
  • Certificate of Registration (CR) and Official Receipt (OR) number
  • Driver’s License
  • Vehicle Make & Model
  • Plate Number
  • Year Model
  • Color
  • Motor/Engine Number
  • Chassis Number
Third Part – Verification Documents:
  • Receipt confirming Php. 50.00 payment
  • Softcopy of valid Certificate of Registration (CR) and Official Receipt (OR)
  • If newly purchased/secondhand and waiting for CR/OR: softcopy of Certificate of Purchase/Ownership from dealer or Deed of Sale from vendor
  • Softcopy of valid Driver’s License (professional/non-professional)
  • If vehicle owned by someone else: valid ID and Authorization Letter from the owner
Is there a fee for registering my vehicle
Yes, there is a one-time fee of Fifty Pesos (Php. 50.00) to be paid at the Cashier’s Office to confirm the payment of the vehicle pass
How do I get my vehicle pass sticker
Complete the online application and upload all the required documents. Then follow these steps:
  • After your application is reviewed and approved, you will be notified through the system to proceed with the payment.
  • Go to the Cashier’s Office to pay the Php. 50.00 fee and get your official receipt.
  • Return to the system and upload a digital copy of the official receipt to finalize your application.
  • Once your payment is validated, bring your vehicle to the PSU Security Services Unit to claim your physical vehicle pass sticker and have it installed.

Managing Your Vehicle Pass

How long is my vehicle pass valid?
The vehicle pass is non-transferable and is valid for one (1) year only from the date of its issuance. It is renewable on or before its expiry date.
How do I renew my vehicle pass?
Renewal of the Vehicle pass requires the same application process, which can be done through the online system. The old sticker must be removed, and the Security Services Unit must install a new one. If you change vehicles, you must bring both the old and new vehicles to the Security Services Unit to remove the old sticker and install the new on the new vehicle.
What if I lose my vehicle pass or have an issue with it?
Replacement of lost, stolen, or damaged stickers shall be at the vehicle owner’s expense
Can I register more than one vehicle?
The guidelines state that only one (1) sticker shall be issued per application. The application form specifies the number of vehicle passes available for faculty/staff and students.

University Policies and Rules

What are the general rules for vehicles inside the campus?
  • Only motorcycles with a stock pipe (not an open pipe) are allowed inside the University.
  • The vehicle owner/driver agrees not to bring prohibited items such as firearms, illegal drugs, or other dangerous chemicals inside the campus.
  • The maximum speed limit inside the campus is 15 km/hr.
  • You must stop for inspection at the gate and during flag ceremonies.
  • All vehicle owners and drivers must adhere to all university rules and policies.
What are the restrictions for motorcycles?
  • Three riders on a motorcycle, including the driver, are not allowed.
  • Motorcycle riders must wear a helmet.
  • Motorcycles with an open pipe are not allowed.
  • The owner of a two-wheeled vehicle (motorcycle) must present their helmet upon entry.
What happens if I violate the guidelines?
The University reserves the right to revoke or suspend entry privileges. Violations such as not wearing a helmet, driving under the influence, or being in possession of illegal items will result in the revocation or suspension of the gate pass. Students who violate the rules may also be liable for disciplinary sanctions under the pertinent provisions of the Student Handbook.
Is the University liable for any losses or damage to my vehicle?
No. The vehicle owner and driver assume full responsibility and liability for any death or injury to persons caused by their vehicle, or for any loss or damage to the vehicle or its items while travelling or parked on University property. The University is not liable for any loss or damage caused by vehicles or due to natural causes or conditions.
